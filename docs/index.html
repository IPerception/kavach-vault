Your passwords.
Your machine. Only.

Kavach is a local-first personal password manager. All credentials are encrypted with AES-256-GCM and stored on your device. No cloud. No accounts. No subscriptions. No one else can access your data -- not even us.

Download for Windows All releases

Windows x64 · No Java required · Eclipse Temurin 21.0.9 bundled

Everything you need. Nothing you don't.

AES-256-GCM Encryption

Every password is encrypted with a unique key. Your master password is never stored -- it derives the encryption key at login.

TOTP-Protected Reveal

Reveal any saved password only after verifying with a 6-digit TOTP code from your authenticator app. Works with Google Authenticator and Authy.

Truly Local

Data never leaves your machine. No sync, no cloud, no remote servers. Your vault is a single SQLite file at ~/.kavach/kavach.db.

Full Audit Log

Every login, credential view, and change is recorded in a tamper-evident audit log so you always know what happened in your vault.

Password Health Report

Instantly see which passwords are weak, reused, or old so you know exactly where to focus your security hygiene.

Encrypted Export & Import

Export your entire vault to an encrypted file for safekeeping or migration. Import it back on any machine running Kavach.

Up and running in minutes

1

Download and extract

Download Kavach-v1.1.0-windows-x64.zip and extract it anywhere on your machine.

2

Run Kavach.exe

Double-click Kavach.exe. Your browser opens automatically to the setup wizard. Create your master password and scan the QR code with your authenticator app.

3

Start adding credentials

Your vault is ready. Add credentials, reveal passwords with TOTP verification, and check your password health report.

Built on open standards

Kavach uses PBKDF2WithHmacSHA256 (600,000 iterations) to derive your master key, AES-256-GCM for all encryption, and RFC 6238 TOTP for credential reveal. The source code is open for review on GitHub. No proprietary algorithms, no hidden backdoors.
